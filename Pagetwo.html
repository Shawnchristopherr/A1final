Personal Profile

Result of online Myers-Briggs test: The Assertive Architect - INTJ-A

The INTJ personality type is defined as analytical problem-solver, innovative, intellectual and introverted.
INTJ’s prefer to work alone in careers that are mentally stimulating, indulging in the challenge.
This is the third rarest personality type on the planet, having only 2% of the population falling under this category.
Famous INTJ personality types include Bill Gates, Stephen Hawking, Elon Musk and Sir Isaac Newton.

What do these results mean for me?

The one thing that I noticed immediately after taking the Myers-Briggs test is that a decent amount of it is hit and miss.
Although it indeed shared insights into some aspects of my personality, it also got a lot of it wrong.
This leads me to believe that even though this is a highly respected and widely used test often relied on by potential employers to sort through candidates, that without pinpoint 100% accuracy the test itself is left with some flaws, and one singularity in this regard by right makes the entirety of the test invalid to be used as scientific evidence, because if one part is not true, what is to say the rest of it is.
Humans are simply much more complex and far too different from one another than to be categorized into subcategories based on the result of a few questions taking the timeframe of mere minutes.
I think it would be more beneficial to take these results as something to take into consideration when dealing with certain situations where this insight may prove useful, using it as a guide rather than taking it as law.
With the results from the learning type tests, I believe this one to be the most accurate of the three.
From past experiences I know that visual learning is where I can concentrate and pick things up quickly, having built several complex things just from the likes of watching YouTube videos and such.

How do I think these results may influence my behaviour in a team?

The result from the testing shows that when working with a team, I will get the most benefit when learning and co-operating through visual means, making use of things like webcam calling and screenshare.
Also, the results suggest that being an INTJ (Architect), I can be a little reclused as I prone to introversion.
This can be remedied by ensuring team communication and participation is actively scheduled and maintained, cementing it into my daily routine, while making consistent efforts to actively ensue the team is all on the same page and each person has their tasks laid out clear to them.

How should you take this into account when forming a team?

When forming a team, it would be vital to try to ensure the rest of my team members all have the same goal and strive to be active and participate actively.
INTJ’s are not overly tolerant of people that do not pick things up as quickly as they do, so putting in steps to make this problem does not surface would be effective.
I also believe getting to know your team during the initial stages will help with communication, as getting to know teammates beyond that of a professional acquaintance will help communication and team-work flow more effortlessly.
