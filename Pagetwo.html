Personal Profile

Result of online Myers-Briggs test: The Assertive Architect - INTJ-A

The INTJ personality type is defined as analytical problem-solver, innovative, intellectual and introverted.
INTJ’s prefer to work alone in careers that are mentally stimulating, indulging in the challenge.
This is the third rarest personality type on the planet, having only 2% of the population falling under this category.
Famous INTJ personality types include Bill Gates, Stephen Hawking, Elon Musk and Sir Isaac Newton.

What do these results mean for me?

The INTJ-A personality type result is interesting, especially when compared with the big five personality test. The results seem to point in accordance with each other, sharing insight into my personality from a logical standpoint I have not thought about yet.
Though naturally, I take these categorisations with a grain of salt, it is fascinating to see how well some of the results for these tests resonate with my personality now.
I believe the key points of the INJT-A to be highly accurate, being Introverted, Innovative, Thinking and Judging. I am a deeply analytical thinker, more often than not using logic to make informed decisions instead of feelings. The introversion trait is also true, often preferring to spend time alone than with others.

How do I think these results may influence my behaviour in a team?

The INTJ personality type is depicted as being deeply analytical thinkers, often seeing outside the box when it comes to problem-solving, and using innovative thinking to create new ideas to overcome and obstacles. This trait can be a valuable part of working within a team, as this will enable the team to have solutions that would not normally be clear to the other personality types.
INTJ’s are also introverted and prefer to work in solitude, this may influence my behaviour in a team by being semi-recluded, and not inclined to contribute for the sake of making friendships, instead only doing so to achieve results.

How should you take this into account when forming a team?

When forming a team, it would be important to keep in mind that communication is a clear part of teamwork, yet too much can exhausting. Keeping conversation focused on the task at hand and avoiding unnecessary small talk is encouraged.
It is also vital for me to recognise if or when I am being reclusive, and to make the effort to communicate more with fellow team members when need be.
